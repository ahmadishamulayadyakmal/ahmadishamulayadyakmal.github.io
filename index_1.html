Who I Am

About Me

I am a metallurgical engineer and LPDP–Tsingshan Group scholar currently pursuing my M.Eng. at the University of Science and Technology Beijing (USTB), one of China's premier institutions for materials science and engineering.

My research focuses on numerical modeling of non-metallic inclusions in continuous casting of heavy rail steel, utilizing ANSYS CFD simulation tools to optimize industrial metallurgical processes. This work sits at the intersection of computational fluid dynamics, materials characterization, and steel quality engineering.

My background spans electrical engineering — including a Q2 Scopus-indexed publication on SMPS flyback topology for LED systems — renewable energy, and AI-assisted technical workflows. I bring interdisciplinary thinking to every challenge, combining hands-on laboratory experience with data-driven approaches.

I am targeting roles in process engineering, R&D, laboratory analysis, and quality assurance within metallurgical and smelting operations, where I can apply both my academic research and industrial experience.
