Who I Am

Bridging Engineering & Scientific Research

A T-shaped engineer growing at the intersection of metallurgy, simulation, and data science.

I am a graduate researcher and LPDP–Tsingshan Group scholarship recipient pursuing a Master of Engineering in Metallurgical Engineering at the University of Science and Technology Beijing (USTB). My thesis focuses on numerical modeling of non-metallic inclusion evolution in continuous casting of heavy rail steel using ANSYS CFD.

My academic journey began in Electrical Engineering at the University of Jember (GPA 3.66/4.00), where I developed a deep interest in power electronics and energy systems — work that led to a Q3 Scopus-indexed publication in the International Journal of Power Electronics and Drive Systems.

Beyond the lab, I have led teams of 17 in demanding field environments in South Kalimantan's mining industry, competed in international innovation competitions, and actively contributed to youth organizations as an active member of PCMI Jawa Timur.

I am targeting process engineering, R&D, laboratory analysis, and quality assurance roles within metallurgical and smelting industries. I am fluent in Indonesian and English, and currently advancing my Mandarin (HSK 3, targeting HSK 5 by 2027).
